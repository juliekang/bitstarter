Educators and parents agree: if you want a kid to pick up a new habit, positive reinforcement and encouragement is the way to go. Librum Vermis! is the next-generation reading incentive platform for kids.

There are existing systems out there that charge schools $500 a student to use their antiquated systems, unnecessarily costing school districts hundreds of thousands of dollars. Librum Vermis! will have questions written by English professors and librarians, personalized emotional encouragement, and look and feel of the modern web...and we will only charge $20 a student. This way, homeschoolers, independent and small schools, as well as public schools can participate in the Librum Vermis adventure!

FAQ

Who are you guys?
We are a team of educators, parents, and developers who want to help kids learn to love reading as much as we do as well as develop a sense of accomplishment and confidence in their learning. At the same time, we want to do this and not over- charge our schools, especially in this time of budget cuts and school closures.
How will the money be spent?
We estimate it will take one developer three months to get this site up and running. Funds will be used to pay the developer and other up-front costs associated with getting the site up. After that, we will charge a yearly fee of $20 per student.
Who is the target audience?
K-12 students, whether they attend public, independent, or home school. Pre-literate children can also participate by logging books that are read to them.
Who will be writing the questions?
We will reach out to English and Library Science professors and graduate students to join our inaugural team of Book Quest! Avengers. They will coordinate and write age- appropriate comprehension questions for 5,000 children's books, as well as give each book a point value dependent on reading level and number of pages. Instead of monetary compensation, we will instead provide them the unique opportunity of joining the thrilling tech start-up world by offering them equity in the company proportional to their participation.
How will you be different from existing programs like Reading Counts and Book Adventure?
We will look very familiar to students who were fortunate enough to attend schools that could afford to subscribe to Reading Counts! However, we do not seek to unnecessarily profit from our struggling school system by charging hundreds of dollars per user! Most importantly, however, the back-end technology will be truly modern and dynamic, so the students' experience will be one of the modern web.
What kind of impact will you make?
We wish to provide that magical incentive to inspire kids to read as much as they can. Once they read a few books they truly enjoy, the incentives will not matter as much, as the habit has been formed. We will continue to encourage them along their reading journey and catalog their accomplishments so they can refer to them at any time. We want to become the main reading incentive platform, reaching tens of millions of students. Join us!
